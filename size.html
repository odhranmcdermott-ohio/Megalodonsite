What Fossils Do We Find?

Most Megalodon fossils are teeth because shark skeletons are mostly cartilage, which does not fossilize as easily as bone.

Why Teeth Matter

Megalodon teeth can grow over 7 inches long. Their size helps scientists estimate body length and feeding habits.

  • Large triangular shape
  • Serrated edges for cutting prey
  • Found in many parts of the world

Fun Feature

Scientists often identify Megalodon by its giant fossil teeth because cartilage rarely survives.
